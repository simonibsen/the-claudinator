# Habits

Cross-cutting practices. Not weekly content — daily/weekly reflexes. Build these and the curriculum compounds. Skip them and it doesn't.

## Daily

- **One commit, even small.** Some days you'll write five lines. Push them. A year of clean commit history is a portfolio.
- **End-of-session note in `learnings.md`.** One paragraph in the project repo: what worked, what didn't, what surprised you. Future-you reads it.
- **Read one diff for fun.** Pick a PR in a project you use. Read it end to end. Even if you don't understand it. Especially if you don't.

## Weekly

- **Friday writeup.** 30 minutes. "What did I learn this module?" Add to your blog or your study repo. By month's end you have 4 posts.
- **One public artifact per month.** Blog post, OSS PR, repo with a real README. Not "a side project I might publish someday" — actually public.
- **Update progress.md.** Has it been a week with no updates? Then you didn't really do anything. Be honest.

## Monthly

- **First Sunday retro.** Look at the month. What stuck? What didn't? What would you cut from the plan? Adjust.
- **Read one research paper.** Doesn't have to be deep. Just one. Build the muscle.
- **Read someone better than you out loud.** Pick a blog post from Simon Willison, Hamel Husain, Eugene Yan. Read it slowly. Notice the moves they make.

## The meta-habit

**Track learning the way you track code.** Your study repo should look like a real project: README, commits, issues for things you're stuck on, PRs even if it's just you, milestones tied to weeks. The discipline transfers.

## Anti-habits to watch for

- **Reading without doing.** Three hours of YouTube tutorials = zero hours of learning. Do the exercise.
- **"Polishing" instead of shipping.** Done > perfect. Always.
- **Skipping the boring modules** (evals, security, design docs). Those are the modules that pay off most over 10 years.
- **Hiding from feedback.** If no one has critiqued your work this month, you don't know how good (or bad) it is.
- **Building only personal stuff.** The OSS streak teaches things solo work can't.
